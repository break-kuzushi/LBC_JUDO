Meet Our Instructors

Our instructors are experienced, passionate, and dedicated to helping you achieve your Judo goals. Learn more about them below.

    Instructor 1: Masahiko Kimura

    Masahiko Kimura was a Japanese judoka and professional wrestler. He is often considered one of the greatest judoka of all time. He was born on September 10, 1917, in Kumamoto, Japan. Kimura began training Judo at the age of 9 and by the age of 15, he had achieved the rank of 2nd dan. He became a 5th dan at the age of 18, which was an impressive feat as it usually takes many years of study and practice to achieve this rank. Kimura was known for his intense and rigorous training regimen, which included thousands of push-ups and hundreds of throws every single day. He won the All Japan Championship at the age of 20 and went on to win it for three consecutive years. Kimura passed away in 1993. His legacy lives on in the martial arts world, and the Kimura, a reverse ude-garami arm lock, is named after him.

    Instructor 2: Kyuzo Mifune

    Kyuzo Mifune was a Japanese judoka who is considered one of the greatest judoka of the 20th century. He was born on April 21, 1883, in Iwate Prefecture, Japan. Mifune started practicing Judo at the age of 13 and joined the Kodokan at the age of 22. He quickly rose through the ranks, achieving the rank of 7th dan at the age of 30. Mifune was known for his technical prowess and his philosophy of 'the flexibility of mind and body.' He was also known for his excellent teaching skills and contributed significantly to the development of Judo through his writings and teachings. Mifune passed away in 1965. His contributions to Judo are still recognized today, and he is often referred to as the 'God of Judo.'

    Instructor 3: Shiro Saigo

    Shiro Saigo was one of the earliest disciples of Judo. He was born on February 4, 1866, in Aizu Wakamatsu, Japan. Saigo, together with Tsunejiro Tomita, became the first in the history of judo to be awarded Shodan by the founder of judo Jigoro Kano, who established the kyu-dan ranking system. He was known for his extreme agility and was nicknamed 'Cat' due to his skill to land on his feet when thrown. He developed a personal technique called 'yama arashi.'
